Basic Techniques

Brush Techniques

Master these fundamental brush techniques for professional results:

  • Load & Dip - Dip brush 1/3 into paint, tap gently on can rim
  • Cutting In - Use angled brush for precise edges around trim
  • Long Strokes - Maintain consistent pressure for smooth finish
  • Feathering - Blend brush marks with light, overlapping strokes

Rolling Techniques

Achieve even coverage with proper rolling methods:

  • W Pattern - Roll in W or M pattern, then fill in
  • Maintain Wet Edge - Work in 4x4 foot sections
  • Even Pressure - Apply consistent pressure throughout stroke
  • Back-Rolling - Go over with light strokes to eliminate texture
Pro Tip: Always work from dry areas into wet paint to avoid lap marks and ensure seamless coverage.

Surface Preparation

Cleaning

Proper surface preparation is crucial for long-lasting results:

  • Remove dirt, grease, and grime with TSP or degreasing cleaner
  • Allow surfaces to dry completely before proceeding
  • Vacuum or dust all surfaces to remove loose particles

Sanding

Smooth surfaces ensure better paint adhesion:

  • 220-grit sandpaper for most surfaces
  • 120-grit for rough or glossy surfaces
  • Sand lightly in circular motions
  • Wipe clean with tack cloth after sanding

Priming

Choose the right primer for your surface:

  • Latex Primer - For most interior walls
  • Oil-Based Primer - For wood and metal
  • Stain-Blocking Primer - For water stains or knots
Remember: Skipping primer may save time initially, but it often leads to poor coverage and color bleeding.

Application Tips

Loading Your Tools

Proper paint loading ensures consistent application:

  • Brushes: Load 1/3 of bristles, tap excess on can rim
  • Rollers: Roll in tray well, remove excess on textured area
  • Sprayers: Maintain consistent distance and speed

Application Order

Follow this sequence for best results:

  • 1. Ceiling - Work in 4x4 sections
  • 2. Walls - Cut in edges first, then roll
  • 3. Trim - Use angled brush for precision
  • 4. Doors & Windows - Paint panels first, then frames

Avoiding Common Mistakes

  • Don't overload brushes or rollers
  • Maintain consistent pressure
  • Work quickly to maintain wet edge
  • Remove tape while paint is still wet

Color & Coverage

Estimating Paint Quantity

Calculate your paint needs accurately:

  • 1 gallon covers approximately 350-400 square feet
  • Measure: Length Ã— Width = Square footage
  • Subtract doors and windows (20 sq ft each)
  • Add 10% extra for touch-ups

Understanding Paint Sheens

Choose the right sheen for each area:

  • Flat/Matte - Ceilings, low-traffic areas
  • Eggshell - Living rooms, bedrooms
  • Satin - Kitchens, bathrooms, hallways
  • Semi-Gloss - Trim, doors, cabinets
  • Gloss - High-traffic areas, furniture

Color Matching & Mixing

  • Test colors in different lighting conditions
  • Use primer tinted close to final color
  • Mix all paint in one large container for consistency
  • Keep color samples for future touch-ups
Color Tip: Colors appear different under various lighting - always test in the actual room first.

Problem Solving

Common Issues & Solutions

Drips & Runs

  • Cause: Too much paint loaded on brush/roller
  • Solution: Sand smooth when dry, spot prime, repaint
  • Prevention: Don't overload tools, maintain proper thickness

Brush Marks

  • Cause: Wrong brush type or technique
  • Solution: Sand lightly, use quality brush, proper technique
  • Prevention: Use appropriate brush for paint type

Uneven Coverage

  • Cause: Insufficient coats or poor primer
  • Solution: Apply additional thin coats
  • Prevention: Use quality primer, don't skip coats

Peeling Paint

  • Cause: Poor surface preparation or moisture
  • Solution: Scrape, sand, prime, repaint
  • Prevention: Proper prep work, address moisture issues
Quick Fix: For small drips, use a dry brush to gently blend while paint is still wet.

Tool Care & Maintenance

Brush Care

Proper maintenance extends brush life significantly:

  • During Use: Don't let paint dry on bristles
  • Cleaning: Use appropriate solvent (water for latex, mineral spirits for oil)
  • Storage: Wrap in paper, store flat or hanging
  • Reshaping: Comb bristles while damp to maintain shape

Roller Care

  • Clean immediately after use
  • Remove excess paint by rolling on cardboard
  • Wash with warm, soapy water
  • Spin dry and store upright

When to Replace Tools

Know when your tools need replacement:

  • Brushes: Bristles fan out, shed excessively, or lose shape
  • Rollers: Fabric tears, sheds lint, or becomes matted
  • Trays: Warped, cracked, or paint buildup affects function
Investment Tip: Quality brushes and rollers last longer and provide better results than cheap alternatives.

Safety Guidelines

Ventilation

Ensure adequate airflow during painting:

  • Open windows and doors for cross-ventilation
  • Use fans to circulate air
  • Avoid painting in enclosed spaces without ventilation
  • Take breaks in fresh air regularly

Personal Protection

  • Eyes: Safety glasses when spraying or working overhead
  • Skin: Wear gloves and long sleeves
  • Respiratory: Use mask for spray painting or sanding
  • Clothing: Wear old clothes or protective coveralls

Ladder Safety

  • Maintain 4:1 ratio (4 feet up for every 1 foot out)
  • Keep three points of contact at all times
  • Don't overreach - move ladder instead
  • Have someone spot you on tall ladders

Chemical Safety

  • Read and follow all product labels
  • Store paints and solvents away from heat sources
  • Dispose of materials according to local regulations
  • Keep products away from children and pets
Emergency Tip: Keep phone numbers for poison control and local emergency services readily available.
